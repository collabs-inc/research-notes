# Malleable Software

[[Geoffrey Litt]]'s thesis that software should be reshapeable by its users at runtime — and that LLMs are the missing technology that finally makes end-user programming real.

## The Idea

From "Malleable software in the age of LLMs" (March 2023):

> "I think it's likely that soon all computer users will have the ability to develop small software tools from scratch, and to describe modifications they'd like made to software they're already using."

> "LLMs will represent a step change in tool support for end-user programming: the ability of normal people to fully harness the general power of computers without resorting to the complexity of normal programming."

Paired with local-first computing, the goal is to "restore user agency" — software you can shape, not just software you rent.

## Why It Matters Here

Malleable software pushes the [[Everyone Is a Builder]] frontier past the product team to the *end user.* In that telling, the job of [[The Product Engineer|product engineering]] becomes building malleable *substrates* rather than fixed features. It rhymes with [[Pete Koomen]]'s "users should edit the System Prompt" and reframes what a product even is when anyone can modify it. A provocative lens for Archetype: the product vision as a living, user-reshapeable artifact.
