# Design Engineer

A hybrid practitioner who makes design decisions *in production code* — the UI specialization of the [[The Product Engineer|product-engineer]] ethos, collapsing the design→frontend handoff into one person.

## Definition & Origin

The modern software usage crystallized ~2020–2022 (Natalya Shelburne et al. 2020, Trys Mudford 2021, Jim Nielsen 2022, David Hoang 2024) and exploded 2023–2024. Two canonical framings:

- **Maggie Appleton:** design engineers "sit squarely at the intersection of design and engineering," able to "quickly iterate by cycling between design exploration, research, and live code."
- **Sean Voisen** (Apr 2024): the three core activities are prototyping experiences, building design-system tooling, and building UIs other engineers "cannot or will not." *"The 'design' in design engineer comes first for a reason."*

## Distinctions

- **vs designer:** primary output is production code, not Figma files. "A product designer asks 'what should we build?' A design engineer asks that *and builds it.*"
- **vs front-end engineer:** "A front-end dev optimizes a render cycle; a design engineer fine-tunes an easing curve." The focus is interaction, motion, and the detail that can't be specified in a handoff.
- **vs [[The Product Engineer|product engineer]]:** the product engineer owns the whole product/business surface; the design engineer owns the *craft* surface.

## Institutionalized at Vercel

[[Vercel]] turned the title into a recruited, well-compensated, first-class role (see its March 2024 *Design Engineering at Vercel* post), then [[Linear]], [[Stripe]], and [[Notion]] followed. A small, identifiable diaspora carries the standard — [[Rauno Freiberg]], [[Paco Coursey]], [[Emil Kowalski]], [[shadcn]], [[Brian Lovin]] — spreading craft through open-source primitives (cmdk, shadcn/ui, Sonner, Vaul) and pedagogy ([[Web Interface Guidelines|Web Interface Guidelines]], Devouring Details, Animations on the Web).
