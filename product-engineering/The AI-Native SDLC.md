# The AI-Native SDLC

The software development lifecycle — idea → prototype → build → ship → measure — compressed and partly automated by AI agents into a tight loop where *humans govern and agents execute.*

## The Framings

- **a16z, "Nine Emerging Developer Patterns for the AI Era"** (Yoko Li, May 2025): the engineer becomes "the orchestrator who decides which thread to pursue, discard, or merge." The load-bearing claim: with coding agents, "code becomes the byproduct… more like a compiled artifact than a manually authored source" — the conceptual hinge linking the new SDLC to [[Spec-Driven Development]].
- **GitHub Octoverse 2025:** "AI, agents, and typed languages are driving the biggest shifts in software development in more than a decade"; positions **2026 as "the year of AI quality"** after 2025's focus on raw speed.
- **"Agentic SDLC" / "AIDLC":** emerging terms (CodeRabbit, Microsoft, AWS, Infosys) for a lifecycle where agents participate across plan → code → review → ship → operate, with "context, memory, and governance as first-class infrastructure."

## The Role Underneath It

The loop's compression is *why* the [[PM–Engineer Convergence|handoffs collapse]] into the [[The Product Engineer|product engineer]]. When one person (plus agents) can carry an idea from discovery to deployment, the org chart follows the workflow. [[Linear]] and [[PostHog]] are the existence proofs — full product orgs built with product engineers and almost no traditional PMs.

The tension the framings surface but don't resolve: who can still understand and govern these systems in two years, when so much code is a generated byproduct? See [[Platform Engineer]] and [[Charity Majors]].
