# Revenue Per Employee

The metric that makes the [[Small Teams, Big Output|small-team]] thesis concrete. Traditional SaaS benchmarks run ~$125K–$200K ARR per employee; the AI-native frontier is now $1M+.

## The Data Points

- **AI labs** (Epoch AI, May 2026): [[Anthropic]] ~$14M and [[OpenAI]] ~$6.5M revenue per employee on headline figures — higher than any public tech company on the Forbes Global 2000 (Nvidia ~$5.1M per its 10-K).
- **[[Anysphere (Cursor)]]:** ~$5M/employee at ~$300M ARR / ~60 staff (early 2025); described by multiple analysts as likely the highest revenue-per-employee company in software history during its $100M–$300M phase.
- **[[Midjourney]]:** ~$5M/employee (~$200M revenue / ~40 staff, 2023) with *no venture capital.*
- **Lovable** (widely cited, secondary): ~$100M ARR with ~45 people (~$2.2M/employee), the fastest SaaS to $200M ARR.
- **[[Ramp]]:** $100M ARR with <5 PMs and ~50 engineers.
- **[[Gumroad]]:** ~$10M ARR, contractor-only, no full-time employees.

> Private-company headcount and ARR vary widely across trackers — treat specific figures as best-available estimates, the pattern as well-attested.

## Why It Matters

Revenue per employee is the scoreboard for "small teams of [[The Product Engineer|product engineers]] outproduce large teams of specialists." It's also the economic engine behind the [[The One-Person Company|one-person company]] and the [[The AI Headcount Mandate|AI headcount mandate]].
