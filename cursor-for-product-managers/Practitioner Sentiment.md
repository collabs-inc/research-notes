# Practitioner Sentiment

How product managers are actually responding to AI tools. Based on surveys, practitioner blog posts, and community discussions — not vendor marketing.

## Adoption: Near-Universal but Shallow

- **100%** of product teams use AI tools (Productboard 2025 survey, n=379)
- **94%** use them daily or often; nearly half say AI is "deeply embedded"
- But only **40%** measure ROI through business outcomes like ARR — most are in the "efficiency gains" phase
- **95%** of enterprise AI pilots fail to deliver measurable ROI (MIT NANDA, July 2025 — 150 interviews, 350 surveys, 300 deployments analyzed)

## What's Working

**Prototyping speed is the clear winner.** Bihan Jiang (Director of Product, Decagon): "Getting something tangible in front of customers used to take weeks. Now I'll start in Claude Cowork...then move into Claude Code to have something demo-able in a couple of hours."

**Data work and synthesis.** Sachin Rekhi: interview synthesis across large numbers of interviews is "exactly where AI shines." Alan Wright: merging JSON files, combining analytics with metadata, cleaning datasets — "Being able to do these little things myself makes me so much faster."

**Aakash Gupta's $28K test:** Tested 47 AI PM tools, kept 9. Claims 44 hrs/week saved across research synthesis (7h), competitive analysis (5.5h), prototyping (18h), AI monitoring (4.5h), and repetitive tasks (9h). The 9 survivors: Dovetail, DeepSky, Mobbin, Reforge Build, Magic Patterns, Linear, Arize, Relay.app, Descript.

## What's Not Working

**Most purpose-built AI PM tools are mediocre.** 38 of 47 in Gupta's test were "overcomplicated, overhyped, overpriced, or overlapping." [[ChatPRD]] scored 65/100 in independent expert analysis despite being the market leader. 67% of PMs say generic AI PRDs need substantial rewriting.

**False precision.** Alan Wright: "AI-generated content shared with the team causes confusion and misalignment because of this false precision." Content looks authoritative even when wrong.

**Tool sprawl.** PMs in 2026 "spend more time managing tools than managing products." 60% use two different tools just for AI prototyping, with no clear market leader.

**Strategy generation is unsolved.** Sachin Rekhi: AI is good at *critiquing* strategy with frameworks but cannot *generate* original strategy. "Anytime I asked Deep Research or ChatGPT to get competitor pricing, the results were woefully out of date."

**Cognitive debt.** HN discussions describe practitioners "getting lost in their own projects and finding it harder to confidently add new features, despite moving faster but losing deeper understanding."

## The "Will AI Replace PMs?" Debate

**Lenny Rachitsky:** AI will have the "most profound impact on the high-level (and historically most valued) skills of product management" — strategy, vision, goal-setting — while soft skills become more valuable. "What are people best at? People stuff!"

**Marty Cagan (SVPG):** Published "Product Coaching and AI" (Feb 2026), described as "a significant change to what we have been advocating for the past two decades." His take: "For many people, their use of AI is simply exposing the theater, rather than highlighting their potential contribution." AI reveals PMs who weren't adding strategic value.

**Austen Allred (opposite view):** "I don't see how the roles of 'product manager' and 'engineer' don't get mashed together in the future. Totally reasonable for a person to figure out what to build and build it with AI."

**A PM client of Ant Murphy:** "I thought AI was going to replace product managers but what's actually happened in practice is we're more important than ever. I can see us moving towards a 1:1 ratio between PM and Engineer."

## Hard Unsolved Problems

1. **Strategy generation vs. critique** — AI can evaluate strategy with frameworks but can't generate original insight
2. **Product sense is experiential** — "AI won't tell you what to build. Product sense comes from direct exposure to customers and markets."
3. **Stated vs. revealed preferences** — "AI can tell you what users said but cannot tell you what users meant"
4. **Speed without direction** — "Speed without direction is just chaos in disguise" (Productboard CPO Survey: 39% of product investments failing due to lack of clear strategy, up from 25%)
5. **Governance vacuum** — 100% of teams use AI tools, only 65% have documented policies
6. **Talent pipeline collapse** — 71% prefer AI-skilled juniors over experienced PMs without AI skills, but only 2% invest in talent development
