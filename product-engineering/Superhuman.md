# Superhuman

The email client that became the reference case for [[Craft|craft]] and obsessive quality as a product strategy.

- **Craft:** Founder Rahul Vohra scrutinizes micro-interactions — animation timing, keyboard shortcuts, copy — and spent ~6 months perfecting typography. Engineering rewrote large parts of Chrome over ~2 years to hit sub-100ms performance. Culture pillars: **Craft, Speed, Delight.**
- **Method:** Built a data-driven **Product-Market Fit Engine** (the "% of users who'd be very disappointed without it" survey) rather than "launch and see what sticks." At peak, ~20 people manually onboarded every new user, freeing engineering to focus on the product.

## Why It Matters

Superhuman shows craft as a deliberate, measurable discipline — the felt quality of every detail as the product, not a finish. It anchors the [[Craft as a Moat|craft-as-moat]] argument alongside [[Linear]] and [[Vercel]], and demonstrates [[Dogfooding|obsessive self-use]] as a quality engine.
