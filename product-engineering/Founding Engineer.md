# Founding Engineer

The earliest technical hire at a startup — often the first employee — defined by *org stage*, not orientation. Builds the MVP, wears non-engineering hats (including de-facto PM, designer, and support), and operates under maximum ambiguity.

## Stage vs. Orientation

A founding engineer is a *stage-bound title*; a [[The Product Engineer|product engineer]] is a *durable orientation*. The product-engineer mindset — end-to-end ownership, customer contact, product judgment — is widely considered the ideal trait *for* a founding engineer, which is why job descriptions for the two increasingly read alike. Gergely Orosz's 2025 market report notes "higher demand for founding engineers and product engineers" together.

## Why Demand Is Rising

As AI compresses how much one person can build (see [[Small Teams, Big Output]] and [[Revenue Per Employee]]), the earliest hires are expected to carry more product and design scope alone. The founding engineer is the startup expression of the [[Everyone Is a Builder]] pattern: fewer people, broader mandates, no handoffs.
