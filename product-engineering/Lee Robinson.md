# Lee Robinson

VP of Developer Experience at Cursor ([[Anysphere (Cursor)]]); previously VP of Developer Experience at [[Vercel]]. Author of the most-cited modern essay naming the [[The Product Engineer|product engineer]].

## The Essay

"Product Engineers" (August 2023). Definition: product engineers *"work backwards from the desired product experience to the set of technologies that enable it."* They "maintain a broad understanding of the available tools and deep experience applying those tools to build products"; the frontend/backend divide is "increasingly obsolete."

> "The tools they use, like React, Svelte, or something else, are secondary. What truly matters is their ability to use these tools to build the best product possible."

His sharpest claim: *"Most startups seeking 'Fullstack Engineers' actually need Product Engineers."* He also drew the [[Platform Engineer|product vs platform]] distinction that organizes the role taxonomy.

## Why It Matters

Robinson supplies the cleanest definition of the role — and his career traces a direct line from the *concept* (the 2023 essay) to a leading agentic tool ([[Anysphere (Cursor)|Cursor]]), where he now leads developer education. He is widely quoted by [[PostHog]] and others.
