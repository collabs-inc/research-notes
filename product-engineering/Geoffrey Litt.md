# Geoffrey Litt

Researcher now at [[Notion]] (previously Ink & Switch; PhD in HCI at MIT under Daniel Jackson); the leading voice on [[Malleable Software|malleable software]] and end-user programming.

## Core Thesis

Software should be reshapeable by its users at runtime, and LLMs are the missing technology that makes end-user programming real. From "Malleable software in the age of LLMs" (March 2023):

> "I think it's likely that soon all computer users will have the ability to develop small software tools from scratch, and to describe modifications they'd like made to software they're already using."

> "LLMs will represent a step change in tool support for end-user programming."

Paired with local-first computing, the aim is to "restore user agency."

## Why It Matters

Litt pushes the [[Everyone Is a Builder]] frontier past the product team to the *end user* — reframing the job of [[The Product Engineer|product engineering]] as building malleable *substrates* rather than fixed features. It's the most provocative long-range lens in the vault for a tool like Archetype: the product vision as a living, user-reshapeable artifact. His projects include Potluck, Embark, and the Ink & Switch malleable-software work.
