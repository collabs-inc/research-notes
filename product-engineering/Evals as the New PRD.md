# Evals as the New PRD

Structured, repeatable, automated tests of AI behavior — "does my AI do the right thing?" — that encode the builder's taste and run continuously. The quality backbone of AI products, and the place where product judgment enters the loop.

## Why Evals Are New

Non-deterministic AI systems can't be verified by traditional assertion-based unit tests. Evals are the substitute. [[Lenny Rachitsky]] calls evals "the first new hard skill in a long time that PMs, engineers, and founders have had to learn — the last one was maybe SQL."

## The Endorsements

- **Garry Tan (YC):** *"Evals are emerging as the real moat for AI startups."*
- **Kevin Weil (OpenAI CPO):** *"Writing evals is going to become a core skill for product managers."*
- **Mike Krieger (Anthropic CPO):** *"If there is one thing we can teach people, it's that writing evals is probably the most important thing."*

## "Evals Are the New PRD"

The eval becomes the spec, the acceptance criteria, and the roadmap at once — teams "hill-climb" against the target on every commit. Hamel Husain & Shreya Shankar (who've trained 2,000+ practitioners): *"Evals let you inject your taste and judgment directly into the product's critical path."*

This is the AI-product expression of [[Taste as the Bottleneck]] and the operational sibling of [[Specs Are the New Code]]: the durable artifact that encodes *what good looks like.*
