# Bending Spoons

The Italian app-portfolio operator (Evernote, WeTransfer, Meetup, Vimeo, and others) that runs a large business with a deliberately lean, [[The Generalist|generalist]]-heavy, "radical simplicity" org.

- **Model:** Autonomous business units per product, each setting its own objectives and methods; flexible engineering process per product maturity ("each team decides on their approach to testing"). **"Radical simplicity"** — complexity must be justified with evidence.
- **People:** Deliberately hires recent grads / less-experienced [[The Generalist|generalists]], selecting for traits over experience. ~500 directly hired staff ("spooners") run the whole portfolio; company-reported ~0.04% acceptance rate and 40–50% adjusted-EBITDA margins (treat as company-stated). Acquisitions are typically followed by large layoffs and consolidation onto shared services.

## Why It Matters

Bending Spoons is the M&A-operator version of [[Small Teams, Big Output]]: small autonomous units, generalists over specialists, an extreme hiring bar — applied to running many products at once rather than building one. A useful contrast to the build-from-scratch case studies.
