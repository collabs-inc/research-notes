# The Generalist

The thesis that AI "raises the floor" on adjacent-domain work, so high-agency generalists now cover ground that used to require whole departments — and that the full-stack generalist has become the most valuable hire for AI-era startups.

## The Claim

Luca Rossi (Refactoring) and others describe a world where "engineers are shipping landing pages, designers are writing ad copy, PMs are running SQL queries, and founders are building features they'd have handed off six months ago." [[The Product Engineer|Product engineers]], [[Design Engineer|design engineers]], and [[Growth Engineer|growth engineers]] are all expressions of the same generalist turn — outcome-owners who span discovery, build, and ship.

[[PostHog]] frames its product engineers as **"T- or M-shaped"** — deep in one area, broad across many. Vipul Shekhawat's definition of the role is openly generalist: "generalists who can take an ambiguous problem statement, define a solution in collaboration with users, and design, implement, and deliver that solution."

## The Counter-Tension

Generalism is not universal. [[Platform Engineer|Platform and infrastructure]] work, deep systems, and the operational lifecycle remain specialist domains ([[Charity Majors]]). The honest version of the thesis: AI expands what one capable generalist can *cover*, while a shrinking core of deep specialists handles what can't be vibe-coded. "Generalists, but not all generalists are product engineers" (Paul Klein).

> Reported hiring signals — e.g. a cited Gartner figure of "+42% YoY demand for AI generalist roles" and LinkedIn's "+74% AI engineering roles" — circulate via secondary blogs; treat the exact numbers as unverified, the direction as well-attested.
