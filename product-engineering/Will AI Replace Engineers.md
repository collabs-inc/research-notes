# Will AI Replace Engineers?

The mirror of [[Will AI Replace Product Managers]], with the same consensus: AI replaces *coding tasks*, not engineering *judgment* — and it pushes surviving engineers *up* into product thinking.

## The Skeptic Core

- **[[Charity Majors]] (Honeycomb), June 2024:** *"Writing code is the easiest part of software engineering, and it's getting easier by the day. The hard parts are what you do with that code — operating it, understanding it, extending it, and governing it over its entire lifecycle."* And: *"Software is an apprenticeship industry."*
- **[[Gergely Orosz]]:** *"most change that AI tools will bring for software engineers are likely to be making the practices that the best eng teams did until now, the baseline."* He names "product-minded engineers, testing, observability, CD" as that baseline — the direct bridge to [[The Product Engineer]].

## The Junior-Developer Problem

- **Steve Yegge, "The Death of the Junior Developer" (June 2024):** LLMs eat the "leaf-node" tasks juniors learned on. *"Chat, it seems, is safer for senior programmers than it is for junior ones."* He later wrote "Revenge of the Junior Developer" as agentic coding matured. See [[The Death of the Junior Developer]].

## The Up-Leveling Thesis

Luca Rossi (Refactoring): the [[The Product Engineer|product engineer]] who "owns entire features, from analysis to post-launch iteration" goes from niche to universal — *"a few years from now, once AI is everywhere, this will likely be what is expected of all engineers."* Paul Klein (2023): "LLMs will give Product Engineers super powers."

## The CEO Signals

[[Tobi Lütke]] ([[Shopify]]) made AI use mandatory before approving headcount; Andy Jassy ([[Amazon]]) told staff to expect a smaller corporate workforce and "scrappier teams"; Marc Benioff (Salesforce) mused about not hiring engineers in 2025 given agent productivity gains. The shared subtext: fewer, more product-minded builders. See [[The AI Headcount Mandate]].
