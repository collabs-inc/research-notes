# Craft

The pursuit of quality in the felt details of a product — typography, motion, timing, the resolution of edge cases — as a discipline in its own right. In this vault, "craft" is one of the two senses of taste (the other being product judgment; see [[Taste as the Bottleneck]]).

## What It Looks Like

- **[[Superhuman]]** — Rahul Vohra spent ~6 months on typography and obsesses over animation timing, shortcuts, and copy; engineering rewrote large parts of Chrome over ~2 years for sub-100ms performance.
- **[[Linear]]** — quality as a deliberate, rare bet; "the details are not the details, they make the product."
- **The [[Design Engineer|design-engineer]] diaspora** — [[Rauno Freiberg]]'s [[Web Interface Guidelines]] and *Devouring Details*, [[Emil Kowalski]]'s motion work (Sonner, Vaul, the *Animations on the Web* course), [[Paco Coursey]]'s cmdk.

## Why It's Strategic Now

Craft is the human signature on AI-assisted output. As generation commoditizes baseline functionality, the perceived quality of a product — how it moves and feels — is one of the few things that doesn't come for free from a model. See [[Craft as a Moat]] for the competitive argument and [[Design Systems and Velocity]] for how craft scales.
