# Midjourney

The AI image-generation company that became the canonical "tiny team, huge revenue, no VC" data point — a working approximation of the [[The One-Person Company|small-company]] frontier.

- **Revenue:** "Significantly surpassed $200M" in 2023 (The Information); secondary trackers estimate ~$300M (2024) and ~$500M (2025).
- **Team:** Famously small — widely repeated as ~11 core members at launch (2022) and ~40 in 2023, implying ~$5M revenue/employee. (Other trackers list 100+; treat the "11 / 40" figures as the popular meme, not firmly sourced.)
- **Funding:** *No outside venture capital;* reportedly profitable since 2022. Founder: David Holz (ex-Leap Motion).

## Why It Matters

Midjourney is the cleanest illustration of [[Revenue Per Employee]] at the frontier and of [[Small Teams, Big Output]]. It anchors the argument that AI-native products can reach enormous scale without the headcount — or the capital — that traditional software required.
