# Shape Up

[[37signals]]' product method — fixed-appetite, shaped work, bet at a betting table and built in six-week cycles by autonomous teams. No sprints, no backlog, no standups. Compiled by Ryan Singer; the free book was published in 2019.

## The Three Phases

1. **Shaping** — senior people define the problem and set an *appetite* (a time budget) plus a rough solution, *before* assigning the work.
2. **Betting** — a single betting-table meeting greenlights what gets built next cycle.
3. **Building** — autonomous teams (designer + programmer) work a six-week cycle, followed by a two-week cooldown.

## Distinctive Mechanics

- **Appetite, not estimate:** fixed time, variable scope — the inverse of estimation (fixed scope, variable time).
- **No shared backlog:** pitches not chosen are simply dropped; unfinished work is cut, not carried over.

## Relevance

Shape Up isn't AI-native, but its discipline — small autonomous teams, fixed time against flexible scope, no backlog — maps cleanly onto AI-accelerated cycles where implementation is cheap and scope can flex. It's a foundational input to [[37signals]]' [[Small Teams, Big Output|small-team]] culture and a frequent reference point for [[The Product Engineer|product-engineer]] teams.
