# Competitive Dynamics

How the [[The Product Engineering Stack|product-engineering stack]] is shaking out — who's converging on the middle of the loop, and where the contested ground is.

## Convergence From Both Ends

The stage most relevant to Archetype — **prototype-from-intent** — is being squeezed from two directions:

- **From design/discovery (left):** [[Figma]] moved from design into prototype and build with **[[Figma Make]]** (prompt-to-app) and Dev Mode; discovery tools push toward specs.
- **From deploy/build (right):** [[Vercel]] pulled left from *ship* into prototype/build with **[[v0]]** ("component generator" → "full-stack app builder"); [[Replit]] spans build-to-deploy with its Agent.

Caught in the middle is a cohort of viral primitives — [[tldraw]] ("make real"), [[Magic Patterns]], [[Subframe]], [[Polymet]], Onlook — each betting on a *spatial / direct-manipulation* interface for product thinking rather than a chat box.

## The "AI Maintainer" Front

As AI writes more code, *review and merge* become the bottleneck. Bessemer named an emerging "AI maintainer" category; [[Graphite]] (AI code review + stacked PRs, Anthropic-backed) is the clearest entrant. This is the *ship* stage maturing into its own contested layer.

## The Strategic Read for Archetype

The build and discovery ends are well-capitalized and consolidating. The defensible position is the **connective product-thinking layer** that no incumbent owns — and, per [[Taste as the Bottleneck]], the layer where the scarce value (judgment, taste) actually lives. The risk mirrors the [[cursor-for-product-managers/Competitive Dynamics|acquisition trap]] in the sibling PM-tools market: point tools get absorbed; an *environment* is harder to bolt on.
