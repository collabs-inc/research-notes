# The Product Engineering Stack

The product engineer's work is a continuous loop — **idea/discovery → prototype → build → ship → measure** — increasingly collapsed into one fast cycle by AI. This is the value-chain framing for the vault's tooling, and the map of where Archetype's white space sits.

## The Loop

| Stage | What happens | Tool categories | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Discovery / idea** | Customer signal, synthesis, opportunity framing | Customer intelligence, research repos, roadmapping | Dovetail, Productboard, Maze — *see [[cursor-for-product-managers/Market Landscape]]* |
| **Prototype** | Prompt/sketch/Figma → clickable or coded prototype | Prompt-to-UI generators, AI design tools | [[tldraw]], [[Magic Patterns]], [[Subframe]], [[Polymet]], [[Figma Make]], [[v0]] |
| **Build** | Generate/edit production code | Agentic coding tools, IDEs, app builders | Cursor, Claude Code, [[Replit]], Bolt, Lovable — *see [[agentic-dev-envs/Market Landscape]]* |
| **Ship** | Deploy, review, merge | Deploy pipelines, AI code review | [[Vercel]], [[Graphite]], Val Town |
| **Measure / iterate** | Analytics, experiments, next bet | Product analytics, experimentation | [[PostHog]], Amplitude |

## The Key Insight

AI *compresses* the loop rather than adding stages — turning "a customer conversation into a working prototype the same day." Discovery (PM tools) and build (ADEs) are each well-funded and deeply tooled. The fragmented, under-owned middle is the *product-thinking connective layer* — prototype-from-intent → measure → next bet. No incumbent owns "an agentic environment for product thinking." That gap is the Archetype thesis.
