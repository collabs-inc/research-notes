# Will AI Replace Product Managers?

The debate has settled into a stable shape: **AI replaces PM *tasks and theater*, not PM *judgment*.**

## The Positions

- **[[Marty Cagan]] (SVPG), "Product Coaching and AI" (Feb 2026):** AI doesn't replace good PMs but *exposes* weak ones. *"For many people, their use of AI is simply exposing the theater, rather than highlighting their potential contribution."* PMs who only produce artifacts are doing work an agent can now do.
- **[[Lenny Rachitsky]] (Aug 2024):** PMs are "best-positioned to thrive" because the job is "an amalgamation of soft skills — communication, collaboration, influence, taste, vision," precisely what AI struggles with. PMs become "editors of super-intelligent suggestions"; the durable value is "people stuff."
- **Sachin Rekhi:** AI's impact is *uneven* — heavy leverage on prototyping and feedback synthesis, near-zero on ambitious vision ("articulating a future that does not yet exist"). The role shifts "from the mechanics of data collection to the strategic work of deriving insights."

## The Benchmark

Lenny Rachitsky & Mike Taylor ran blind polls of AI vs. human PM answers (July 2024): AI won product strategy (55%) and metrics (68%), humans narrowly won ROI estimation (58%). Lenny: *"Closer than I expected… right now is the worst AI will ever be at any task."*

## The Market Reality

Despite "AI kills the PM" headlines, openings hit a multi-year high — Lenny's tracker reported **7,300+ open PM roles globally in early 2026**, ~75% above the early-2023 low (a later May 2026 reading put the broader figure near 24,900, growth concentrated at senior levels). The pain is concentrated at the *junior* level and in support/ops/mid-management, not senior product work.
