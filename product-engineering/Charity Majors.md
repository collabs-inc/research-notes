# Charity Majors

Co-founder and CTO of Honeycomb; a leading skeptic of the "AI replaces engineers" narrative and a key voice on what engineering work actually *is*.

## Core Thesis

Writing code is the easy part; the durable work is operating, understanding, and governing systems over their lifecycle. From "Generative AI is not going to build your engineering team for you" (June 2024):

> "Writing code is the easiest part of software engineering, and it's getting easier by the day. The hard parts are what you do with that code—operating it, understanding it, extending it, and governing it over its entire lifecycle."

> "Software is an apprenticeship industry. You can't learn to be a software engineer by reading books."

## Why It Matters

Majors is the strongest counterweight to [[Everyone Is a Builder]] triumphalism, and her "apprenticeship" point names the real risk in [[The Death of the Junior Developer]]: if AI removes the junior rung, the pipeline that produces senior [[The Product Engineer|product engineers]] breaks. She also argues 2025 was the tipping point where AI became foundational dev tooling (the way cloud did ~2010) — so she is a skeptic of the *hype*, not of AI's importance. Her domain (observability, operating systems in production) is exactly the [[Platform Engineer|specialist]] work the [[The Generalist|generalist]] sweep doesn't reach.
