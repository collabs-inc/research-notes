# Anthropic

The AI lab (maker of Claude and Claude Code) notable here for two things: an org model that deputizes product-minded engineers as "mini-PMs," and a counterintuitive scaling response to its own productivity gains.

- **"Mini-PMs":** For any project under ~two weeks of engineering time, product-minded engineers run their own scope, make product decisions, and ship without a formal PM. (Sourced via interviews with CPO Mike Krieger — verify exact wording.)
- **"Hire more PMs, not more engineers":** As Claude Code lifts engineering output (the claim: 5 engineers produce the work of 15–20), the binding constraint shifts to *deciding what to build* — so the marginal hire is a PM. This is [[PM-to-Engineer Ratios]] inverted by extreme dev productivity.
- **Leadership:** Mike Krieger (Instagram co-founder) joined as CPO in 2024; PMs partner with research, product engineering, and platform engineering, and research-PM roles require deep technical depth (Python, SQL).

## The Efficiency Signal

Epoch AI (May 2026) estimated Anthropic at roughly **$14M revenue per employee** — higher than any public tech company on the Forbes Global 2000. See [[Revenue Per Employee]].

## Why It Matters

Anthropic is a live experiment in what happens when a team's coding throughput is multiplied by its own tools: the work doesn't disappear, it migrates *upstream* to product judgment — exactly the [[Taste as the Bottleneck]] thesis. It also [[Dogfooding|dogfoods]] aggressively, building its products with its own agents.
