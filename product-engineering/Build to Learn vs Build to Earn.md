# Build to Learn vs Build to Earn

[[Marty Cagan]] / SVPG's framing for keeping prototypes and products distinct: *"Product discovery is all about building to learn, and product delivery is all about building to earn."*

## The Distinction

- **Build to learn** — [[Continuous Discovery|discovery]]: ephemeral prototypes built to validate an idea fast. Quality and durability don't matter; learning does.
- **Build to earn** — delivery: production-quality work customers depend on.

## Why It Matters More in the AI Era

Cagan: *"As the cost of product delivery continues to drop, the bottleneck and competitive advantage moves to product discovery… The hard part is rarely building; the hard part is discovering the right solution to build."* And: *"Vibe coding is putting product discovery on steroids."*

The danger AI introduces: it becomes tempting to ship a "build-to-learn" [[Vibe Coding|vibe-coded]] prototype as if it were "build-to-earn." The distinction is the discipline that keeps fast prototyping from becoming fragile production. It's the same boundary [[Augmented Coding]] and [[Spec-Driven Development]] enforce on the engineering side.

Cagan is the author of *INSPIRED*, *EMPOWERED*, and *TRANSFORMED: Moving to the Product Operating Model* (2024).
