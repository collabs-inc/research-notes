# Prototyping as Thinking

Build a rough artifact to *think with* and *show* — prototypes generate insight and align stakeholders better than documents. The practice most transformed by AI.

## The Lineage

- **"Build to think"** — the design-thinking tradition (IDEO, Stanford d.school): prototypes let you "fail quickly and cheaply" and reason in tangible rather than abstract terms.
- **"Demo or die"** — Nicholas Negroponte, founding director of the MIT Media Lab (1985); later evolved by Joi Ito to "deploy or die" as deployment costs fell.
- **"If a picture is worth a thousand words, a prototype is worth a thousand meetings."** — attributed to Tom Kelley, IDEO.

## What AI Changed

[[Vibe Coding|Vibe-coding]] and AI prototyping tools ([[v0]], Bolt, Lovable, Replit, Cursor, [[tldraw]], [[Figma Make]]) turn "build to think" from sketches into *interactive, live-data prototypes in hours.* [[Marty Cagan]] reports teams making "10–20 (or more) prototypes per week" as normal. Two consequences:

1. The prototype increasingly *is* the spec and the pitch.
2. It pulled non-engineers directly into building — [[Teresa Torres]] (a [[Continuous Discovery]] researcher with no engineering background) began vibe coding in 2025 and shipped a production tool, calling it "particularly powerful for assumption testing."

The discipline to remember: a prototype is [[Build to Learn vs Build to Earn|build-to-learn]], not build-to-earn.
