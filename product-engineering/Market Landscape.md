# Market Landscape

Sizing and structure for "product engineering" as a market — which is, as of June 2026, less a named category than an *intersection* of two profiled markets plus a thin, emerging connective layer.

## There Is No Named "Product Engineering Tools" Market Yet

No analyst has named a clean "product engineering tools" category. It currently reads as the overlap of:

- **Agentic dev environments** ("vibe coding," ADEs) on the build side — see [[agentic-dev-envs/Market Landscape]].
- **PM-discovery tooling** on the discovery side — see [[cursor-for-product-managers/Market Landscape]].
- A thin connective layer of prototype-from-intent tools ([[tldraw]], [[Magic Patterns]], [[Polymet]], [[Figma Make]], [[v0]]).

## Naming-Collision Warning

"**Product Engineering Services**" is an existing, unrelated IT-outsourcing category (outsourced design/dev/test, often hardware/embedded), sized at ~$1.3T (2025) → ~$1.8T by 2030. The "$1.48T product engineering market" figure circulating in some blogs is *this outsourcing market, misapplied* to the software-role trend. Do not conflate.

## The Closest VC Framing

Bessemer's "Roadmap: Developer Tooling for Software 3.0" (Aug 2025): "Natural language becomes the primary programming interface and AI agents… evolve into developers themselves," turning "developers into anyone who can articulate a vision." It predicts "95%+ of code AI-written by 2030" in high-velocity companies and names an emerging **"AI maintainer"** category (where [[Graphite]] sits) — but maps tools by infrastructure layer, not by the product loop.

## The Scale Signal

The "anyone builds software" wave is real and capitalized: [[Anysphere (Cursor)|Cursor]] (>$2B ARR, ~$29B valuation), Lovable (fastest SaaS to $200M ARR), [[Replit]] (~$9B valuation). The labor-market and [[Revenue Per Employee|revenue-per-employee]] signals are covered in [[Labor Market]] and [[Small Teams, Big Output]].
