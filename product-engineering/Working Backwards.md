# Working Backwards

[[Amazon]]'s practice of writing the press release and FAQ for a product *before building it* — and the six-page narrative memo that replaced PowerPoint. The original "writing as product thinking."

## The Tools

- **The PR/FAQ** — start from the desired customer experience, write a mock press release plus an FAQ. If you can't explain why it matters to customers, you don't build it. AWS (S3/EC2) reportedly went through a year-plus of PR/FAQ iteration before its 2006 launch.
- **The six-pager** — narrative memos replaced slide decks in meetings starting ~2004, read silently at the start of the meeting. The forcing function: prose exposes fuzzy thinking that bullet points hide.

Codified in Colin Bryar & Bill Carr's *Working Backwards* (2021).

## Why It Belongs in the AI Era

Writing-as-thinking is enjoying a revival. The PR-FAQ is structurally the ancestor of the [[Spec-Driven Development|spec]] and the [[Specs Are the New Code|"specs are the new code"]] thesis — a precise English document that forces clarity and now *compiles* into product. The durable artifact connecting [[Amazon]] narratives (2004), executable specs (2025), and [[Evals as the New PRD|eval-as-PRD]] is the same: clear writing about intent.
