# The AI Headcount Mandate

The top-down forcing function accelerating the [[The Product Engineer|product-engineer]] shift: executives making AI fluency a condition of employment and a prerequisite for new hires.

## The Memos

- **[[Tobi Lütke]] ([[Shopify]]), April 2025:** *"Reflexive AI usage is now a baseline expectation at Shopify."* And the line that traveled furthest: *"Before asking for more headcount and resources, teams must demonstrate why they cannot get what they want done using AI."* AI competency was added to performance and peer reviews.
- **Andy Jassy ([[Amazon]]), June 2025:** *"We will need fewer people doing some of the jobs that are being done today, and more people doing other types of jobs… we expect that this will reduce our total corporate workforce."* Teams should learn to "get more done with scrappier teams."
- **Marc Benioff (Salesforce), 2025:** *"In engineering this year… we're seriously debating, maybe we aren't going to hire anybody this year because we have seen such incredible productivity gains because of the agents."* Salesforce later confirmed ~4,000 support-role cuts "because I need less heads."

## The Effect

The mandate turns AI adoption from optional to structural. Combined with [[Small Teams, Big Output|small-team]] economics and rising [[Revenue Per Employee]], it pushes orgs toward fewer, broader, more product-minded builders — and away from the narrow ticket-taking roles most exposed to agents.

> Layoff context: ~95K tech layoffs in 2024, ~123K+ in 2025, widely characterized as "surgical" — cutting support/ops/mid-management while hiring AI/ML talent — rather than blanket reduction.
