# Retool

The internal-tools platform founded with an all-engineer team — an early example of "engineers who are their own product managers because they are their own customer."

- **Model:** Founded with an all-engineer team that had "knowledge of both product and market"; engineer-first, highly iterative, customer-driven. The founders were their own customer, which gave them "good taste in strategic decisions." Every employee builds and uses Retool internally ([[Dogfooding|dogfooding]]).

> Note: public sourcing is thin on Retool's *current* PM structure — this note does not assert a "no PMs" claim, only the all-engineer founding pattern.

## Why It Matters

Retool illustrates the founding case for the [[The Product Engineer|product engineer]]: when the builders *are* the users, product judgment and engineering live in the same people, and the PM handoff never forms. David Hsu's framing — taste in strategic decisions coming from being the customer — is a small-scale version of [[Continuous Discovery]] collapsed into the build team.
