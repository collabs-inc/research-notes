# Anysphere (Cursor)

The maker of Cursor, the AI code editor — included here as the extreme case of [[Small Teams, Big Output|small-team]], high-[[Revenue Per Employee|revenue-per-employee]] output, and as the employer of [[Lee Robinson]], who wrote the canonical [[The Product Engineer|product-engineer]] essay.

- **Growth (Contrary Research, Dec 2025):** ARR ~$100M (Jan 2025, ~20 months after launch) → ~$300M (Apr 2025) → ~$500M (June 2025); secondary sources report $1B+ later in 2025 and ~$2B by early 2026.
- **Headcount:** ~60 employees (Mar 2025) → ~245 (Dec 2025) per Contrary. (Other trackers disagree widely — treat non-Contrary figures cautiously.) This implies ~$5M revenue/employee in early 2025 — described by multiple analysts as likely the highest in software history during the $100M–$300M phase.
- **Funding:** ~$3.5B raised; Series D ~$2.3B at a ~$29.3B valuation (Nov 2025).

## Why It Matters

Cursor is the scale proof for "a tiny team of [[The Product Engineer|product engineers]] outproduces a large org." Its "0→$100M ARR in ~21 months with ~20 people" is one of the most-cited [[Revenue Per Employee|revenue-per-employee]] stories of the era. (The product itself — the editor — is profiled in the sibling vault [[agentic-dev-envs/Cursor]].)
