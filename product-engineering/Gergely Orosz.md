# Gergely Orosz

Author of *The Pragmatic Engineer* (the largest technology newsletter on Substack) and ex-engineering manager at Uber, Skype, and Skyscanner. The person who codified the [[The Product-Minded Software Engineer|product-minded software engineer]] — the foundational concept of this vault.

## Core Thesis

An engineer who carries product thinking on top of engineering skill, shapes what gets built, and owns features end-to-end. His 2019 essay (see [[The Product-Minded Software Engineer]]) predates the AI wave and lists the nine traits everyone now cites; it became an O'Reilly book in 2025.

## On AI and the Role

Orosz's position bridges the two debates: AI doesn't replace engineers, it raises the floor. *"Most change that AI tools will bring for software engineers are likely to be making the practices that the best eng teams did until now, the baseline… Things like product-minded engineers, testing, o11y, CD."* His 2025 market reporting notes rising demand for "founding engineers and product engineers," and he documents the field closely (interviews with [[Kent Beck]], [[Charity Majors]], and [[DHH]]'s shift to agent-first coding).

## Why It Matters

Orosz is both the *origin* of the product-engineer concept and one of its most credible ongoing chroniclers. He also coined the modern framing of the [[Forward-Deployed Engineer]] for a broad audience.
