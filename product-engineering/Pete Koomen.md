# Pete Koomen

General Partner at Y Combinator and co-founder of Optimizely (YC W10); author of the widely-shared essay "AI Horseless Carriages" (April 2025).

## Core Thesis

Most AI apps are "horseless carriages" — AI bolted onto legacy software designs instead of software rebuilt around AI. Crucially, **users, not developers, should control the system prompt**:

> "Most AI apps should be _agent builders_, not agents."

> "When an LLM agent is acting on my behalf I should be allowed to teach it how to do that by editing the System Prompt."

His canonical failure case is Gmail's AI draft feature: generic, formal, longer than users would write, with no access to the system prompt.

## Why It Matters

Koomen shares [[Geoffrey Litt]]'s bet that AI's real unlock is letting *end users* reshape software — pushing the "builder" frontier past the product team. For [[The Product Engineer|product engineers]] and tools like Archetype, the design lesson is to build *configurable substrates* users can teach, not fixed agents. See [[Malleable Software]] and [[Everyone Is a Builder]].
