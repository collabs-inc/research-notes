# Dogfooding

Using your own product internally as a real user — the ultimate confidence test before and during shipping.

## Origin

The consumer roots are 1970s Alpo dog-food ads; the tech adoption is Microsoft, 1988, when manager Paul Maritz emailed "Eating our own Dogfood," challenging the team to use Microsoft products. Reinforced by Dave Cutler's insistence (1991) on building Windows NT on daily NT builds. Mozilla popularized the term in the 1990s.

## Why It Belongs Here

Dogfooding is a [[The Product Engineer|product-engineer]] practice by nature — it collapses the distance between builder and user, which is the whole point of the role. The companies in this vault dogfood aggressively: [[Retool]] runs on Retool, [[Figma]] is designed in Figma, and the AI labs ([[Anthropic]], [[OpenAI]]) build their products with their own coding agents. That last form is also the implicit method behind the [[Evals as the New PRD|eval]] claims — leaders judge their models by building real products on them.
