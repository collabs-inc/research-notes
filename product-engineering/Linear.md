# Linear

The project-management tool that is also the field's favorite *existence proof*: a respected product org built on [[The Product Engineer|product engineers]] and [[Craft as a Moat|craft]], with almost no PMs.

- **Funding/scale:** ~$1.25B valuation (Series C, 2025); ~87 employees at that stage; 18,000+ paying customers; effectively profitable since ~2021.
- **PMs:** One Head of Product (Nan Yu), hired at ~25 people; ~2 PMs total. Saarinen: *"Instead of having a PM for every team or area, we like to see that the broader team collectively thinks about the product."* Project leads can be engineers or designers — "never a PM."
- **Teams:** No durable cross-functional teams. Ephemeral project pods (~1 designer + 2 engineers) form around work and disband on completion. Only two permanent teams (mobile, infrastructure).

## Taste Over Metrics

Linear **rejects A/B testing.** Karri Saarinen: *"We validate ideas and assumptions that are driven by taste and opinions, rather than the other way around where tests drive decisions."* Opinionated design: "we design it so that there's one really good way of doing things" (Jori Lallo). One company-level North Star, no per-project metric goals.

## Why It Matters

Linear is cited everywhere in this vault — by [[PostHog]] as a product-engineer org, by the [[Craft as a Moat|craft]] discussion (quality as deliberate strategy, near-zero marketing spend, word-of-mouth growth), and as the home of design engineers [[Paco Coursey]] and [[Emil Kowalski]]. It is the proof that "engineers and designers can be their own PMs" at real scale.
