# Aha!

Broadest feature surface among incumbents but shallowest AI. Bootstrapped, ~3K customers.

## AI Capabilities

- **Elle** — AI assistant embedded throughout the suite. Drafts strategy documents, synthesizes research, generates wireframes from text/images, creates working prototypes, sends requirements to coding agents (GitHub Copilot, Cursor, Claude Code, Codex, Devin).
- **Aha! Discovery** ($39/user/mo standalone, $59/user/mo with other Aha! products) — dedicated product discovery module. AI analyzes customer interviews, surfaces highlights, generates JTBD reports and voice-of-customer analyses. 8 insight report types.
- **Research agent** (Q1 2026) — reviews records, documents, and trusted web sources for comprehensive analysis.
- **Custom AI agents** — organization-specific agents with tailored instructions.
- **One-click "send to coding agent"** — notable bridge to execution, but it's a handoff, not an integrated loop.

## Relevance to "Cursor for PMs"

Theoretical path from research to insights to strategy to specs to prototype exists. But the AI is primarily a writing assistant — it generates drafts from prompts rather than autonomously detecting patterns or making recommendations. The tool's complexity works against it. Won a bronze Edison Award for Discovery (vendor recognition, not user validation).

## User Reception

G2: ~4.3/5. Praised for customization and strategic planning depth. Criticized for: dated UI, steep learning curve, complexity overwhelming for smaller teams, pricing that escalates quickly ($100+/user/mo for full suite), and basic quality-of-life issues (can't tag colleagues in notes, limited table sorting).
