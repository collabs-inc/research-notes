# PostHog

The poster child of [[The Product Engineer|product-engineer]] culture. PostHog (product analytics / dev-tools platform) brands itself as making "dev tools for product engineers" and maintains a public **Product Engineer Handbook**.

- **Model:** "A wide company with small teams." Each "one-pizza team" of **2–6 people** owns a product area and "behaves like an early-stage startup," owning usage, quality, and revenue.
- **PMs:** Engineers own product decisions, sprint planning, and specs. PMs do *not* manage teams or engineers; they support the small teams with prioritization, research, and competitor analysis, and "coach product engineers on how to do product." A product engineer is never on more than one team.
- **Hiring:** "Hell, yes!" or "hell, no." *"The rot begins the moment you compromise and hire someone who isn't a 'hell, yes!'"*

## On the Role

PostHog's definitions are among the most-cited in the field:

- *"Product engineers focus on building great products while software engineers focus on building reliable software."* (see [[Product Engineer vs Software Engineer]])
- *"Product engineers are a hybrid of software engineer and product manager."* (see [[Product Engineer vs Product Manager]])
- In "Product management is broken. Engineers can fix it" (Dec 2024): *"PMs exist to empower the engineers, not control them… They don't decide the destination."*

PostHog also publishes the demand signal most often quoted for the role: an **89% rise in searches for "product engineers" since 2021** (a search-interest proxy, not a postings count), and cites [[Linear]] as "built almost entirely by product engineers."

## Why It Matters

PostHog is building the *tooling-and-identity flywheel* for the title — it both employs the model and evangelizes it. It is the clearest worked example of [[Small Teams, Big Output]] and [[PM–Engineer Convergence]].
