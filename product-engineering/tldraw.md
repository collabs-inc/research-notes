# tldraw

An infinite-canvas SDK whose viral "make real" demo turns a drawn UI into working code — the archetypal "sketch → real software" primitive, and a bet on a *spatial* interface for product thinking rather than a chat box.

- **"make real"** (Nov 2023) used a vision model to interpret a hand-drawn UI into functioning Tailwind + JavaScript.
- **Funding:** ~$10M Series A (April 2025), led by Lux Capital and Definition; ~$12–14M total. The SDK powers whiteboards inside ClickUp, Padlet, Jam, and others; tldraw.computer is a later collaboration with Google DeepMind.

## Why It Matters

tldraw is proof that "draw the product, get the product" resonates — a visual/combinatorial surface for ideation that's directly adjacent to a product-thinking environment. It's one of the clearest examples of the prototype-from-intent layer in [[The Product Engineering Stack]], and of the spatial-canvas alternative to chat-based building (alongside Onlook).
