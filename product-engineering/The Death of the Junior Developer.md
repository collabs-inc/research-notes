# The Death of the Junior Developer

Steve Yegge's June 2024 Sourcegraph essay, the sharpest articulation of AI's *uneven* threat to engineering: the danger is concentrated at the entry level, not the senior level.

## The Argument

LLMs now handle the routine "leaf-node" coding tasks historically assigned to juniors — exactly the tasks juniors learned the craft on. *"Chat, it seems, is safer for senior programmers than it is for junior ones."* Seniors have the judgment to catch "specious" bad guidance; juniors don't. Yegge's analogy: "Catching the LLM in a hallucination, catching a junior associate in a mistake — pretty similar," except one is "fast and tireless and essentially free."

His advice to juniors: bank experience before employment (OSS, internships), master fundamentals, and adopt "chat-oriented programming" aggressively.

## The Tension With Apprenticeship

This collides with [[Charity Majors]]'s point that "software is an apprenticeship industry" — if AI removes the junior rung, the pipeline that produces seniors breaks. The unresolved question: where do tomorrow's senior product engineers come from if the entry-level learning ground is automated?

## The Sequel

Yegge later wrote *"Revenge of the Junior Developer,"* arguing that in the agentic-coding era, the developers most fluent at orchestrating fleets of agents — often younger ones — gain an edge. The framing moved from threat to inversion.
