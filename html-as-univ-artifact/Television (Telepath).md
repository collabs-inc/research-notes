# Television (Telepath)

The product at **television.run** — a "GUI for personal agents" where an agent creates, pins, and organizes **persistent artifacts** (cards, interactive views, and web pages) that survive across chat sessions. The single most relevant item in this vault to Archetype's premise: *the artifact, not the chat, is the unit of work.*

> Note: despite the name, television.run is **not** a generative-TV experiment — it's the marketing site for Television, by the startup Telepath.

## What It Is (from the site)

- *"Television gives you and your agent a visual space for creating and working with artifacts"* — billed as *"the missing GUI for personal agents."*
- **Artifacts:** *"let your agent pin important information in Television instead of losing it in chat"* — "simple cards, interactive views, or external web pages that you revisit and organize over time." (This is the HTML-as-artifact link: the agent's output is a persistent web/interactive view, not ephemeral text.)
- **Screens:** persistent spaces to "collect, arrange, and return to the things you're working on."
- **Skills:** turn favorite artifacts into "reusable instructions for creating interfaces" the agent can adapt or share.
- **Distribution:** "Television ships as a skill. If your agent can run commands and write files, it can use Television." Listed compatible harnesses: OpenAI Codex, OpenCode, Hermes, Claude Code, OpenClaw.

## Who's Building It

Telepath (telepath.computer), founded by ex-Mozilla colleagues: **Stephen Hood** (CEO; previously led open-source AI at Mozilla, founded Llamafile), **Josh Whiting** (CTO), and **Rupert Manfredi** (CDO; previously ran the open-source Unternet project). Introduced ~Oct 2025; went through Betaworks Camp. Television is in **alpha, with an open-source release stated as coming.**

Verified backers (per Hood's own announcement): **Betaworks, True Ventures, Mozilla Ventures, RRE, Fermont Capital**, plus angels **Joshua Schachter** (del.icio.us), **Thomas Wolf** (Hugging Face), and **Ari Weinstein** (OpenAI). No funding amount is publicly disclosed. (Widely-circulated "$20M / USV / Lightspeed" figures are a confabulation — do not cite them.) Manfredi's broader design vision describes "a magnetic 3D space of cards, upon which generative interfaces are rendered" — though the rendering stack and model are not publicly documented.

## Why It Matters

Television treats the agent's deliverable as a **durable, organizable artifact** rather than disposable chat output — the "persistent HTML artifact as the unit of work" thesis applied to a personal-agent OS. Hood frames today's AI products as "hybrid cars" (AI bolted onto a 40-year-old interface) and Telepath as building "EVs." This is the closest external project to Archetype's own bet: an environment where the artifact is the product.
