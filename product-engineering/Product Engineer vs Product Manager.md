# Product Engineer vs Product Manager

Same goals, different method. The clearest articulation comes from [[PostHog]] (Ian Vanagas, Jan 2023): *"Product engineers are a hybrid of software engineer and product manager. They have the same goals as product managers, but they ship code and approach problems in a more direct, iterative way."*

## The Distinction

| | Product Engineer | Product Manager |
|---|---|---|
| Primary output | Shipped code / working product | Specs, alignment, prioritization |
| Method | Build → ship MVP → iterate on direct feedback | Research → spec → stakeholder alignment → orchestrate |
| Decisions | Makes product decisions autonomously | Provides context, coordinates across teams |
| Best when | Small, fast, engineering-led teams | Organizational complexity at scale |

## The PM, Reframed

In product-engineer-led orgs, the PM is not deleted but redefined as a *context-provider and coach* rather than a roadmap owner:

- [[PostHog]]: PMs "don't manage product teams or engineers"; they "coach product engineers on how to do product" and ensure owners have company, industry, and customer context. James Hawkins: *"PMs exist as the team's compass. They don't decide the destination."*
- [[Linear]] runs with one Head of Product (Nan Yu) and ~2 PMs across the company; project leads are engineers or designers.
- [[Stripe]]'s explicit rule of thumb: *"The more technical and backend heavy your product, the fewer product managers you will have."*

The counter-evidence: PM hiring sits at multi-year highs (see [[Will AI Replace Product Managers]]). The role is being *reshaped*, not erased.
