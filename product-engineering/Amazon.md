# Amazon

The source of the classic org primitives that the [[The Product Engineer|product-engineer]] era keeps re-deriving: small teams, single ownership, and writing-as-thinking.

## The Primitives

- **Two-pizza teams:** no team larger than two pizzas can feed (ideally <10), to minimize communication overhead.
- **Single-threaded ownership:** Amazon found the decisive factor wasn't team size but a **single-threaded leader** owning one product across its full lifecycle — no hand-offs. "Team leaders help remove obstacles, not act as a barrier all decisions pass through."
- **[[Working Backwards]]:** start from the desired customer experience; write the press release and FAQ (and the six-page narrative memo) *before* building.

Bezos: *"Setting the bar high in our approach to hiring has been, and will continue to be, the single most important element of Amazon.com's success."*

## Why It Matters

Single-threaded ownership and the two-pizza team are the ancestors of [[PostHog]]'s "one-pizza teams" and [[Small Teams, Big Output]]. [[Working Backwards]] is the ancestor of [[Spec-Driven Development|specs]] and [[Specs Are the New Code|"specs are the new code"]]. And Andy Jassy's 2025 memo on a smaller, "scrappier" corporate workforce ([[The AI Headcount Mandate]]) shows the same company applying these primitives to the AI era.
