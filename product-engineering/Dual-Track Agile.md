# Dual-Track Agile

Two parallel, continuous tracks — **Discovery** (figure out *what* to build) feeding **Delivery** (build it well) — instead of a sequential spec-then-build process.

## Lineage

The term "dual-track" was coined by Jeff Patton and popularized by [[Marty Cagan]] / SVPG (the two spoke on "dual-track scrum" around 2012). The earliest root is Lynn Miller (Alias, 2005), who described "interconnected parallel design and development tracks." Discovery's output is Delivery's input; the two run continuously, not in phases.

## What AI Changes

As delivery cost collapses, the *balance* between the tracks shifts toward discovery. This is Cagan's [[Build to Learn vs Build to Earn]] point: "as the cost of product delivery continues to drop, the bottleneck and competitive advantage moves to product discovery." The [[The Product Engineer|product engineer]] increasingly runs both tracks — discovering and delivering without a handoff between them.
