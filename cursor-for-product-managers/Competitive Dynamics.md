# Competitive Dynamics

How the "Cursor for Product Managers" market is shaping up structurally.

## The Four Tiers

**Tier 1 — Incumbents adding AI:** [[Productboard]] ($262M raised, $1.72B valuation), [[Atlassian Jira]] (acquired Cycle, Rewatch), Pendo ($468M raised, acquired Chisel Labs), [[Aha!]] (bootstrapped, 3K customers), Amplitude (public, acquired [[Kraftful]] + [[Inari]])

**Tier 2 — AI-native startups (venture-backed):** [[Enterpret]] ($25.1M Series A, clients include Notion/Canva/Linear), [[Unwrap.ai]] ($12M Series A, clients include Microsoft/Perplexity/JetBlue), [[BuildBetter]] ($4.05M, clients include OpenAI/Brex), Chattermill ($37M total, clients include Amazon/Uber), [[Sprig]] ($152M raised, ~$300M valuation), [[Dovetail]] ($69.5M raised, $4.9M ARR), [[Maze]] ($20.4M ARR)

**Tier 3 — Focused/emerging:** [[ChatPRD]] (bootstrapped, 100K users), Keeborg (8-doc spec suite in 90 seconds), Canny (bootstrapped, 7K companies), Featurebase, Looppanel ($1M pre-seed), BluePill ($6M seed for synthetic consumer panels), IdeaPlan/Forge

**Tier 4 — Acquired/dead:** See [[Market Landscape]] for full list

## Key Dynamics

**Incumbents absorb, they don't build.** Atlassian acquired Cycle rather than building feedback AI internally. Amplitude acquired both [[Kraftful]] and [[Inari]]. Pendo acquired Chisel. The pattern is consistent: buy the discovery startup, integrate into the existing execution platform.

**Execution tools don't want to become discovery tools.** [[Linear]] explicitly stays in its lane. [[Notion]] is a platform, not an opinionated workflow. This creates a structural opening for a tool that owns [[The Full Loop Gap|the full loop]].

**Credit-based AI pricing is universal and universally disliked.** [[Productboard]] ($15/mo + credits), [[Notion]] ($10/1K credits), [[Atlassian Jira]] (included but with coming overage billing), [[Aha!]] (pooled credits). Unpredictable costs are a consistent complaint. A simpler pricing model could differentiate.

**No one owns the spec-to-agent handoff.** The RFS vision ends with development tasks ready for coding agents. Today, [[Aha!]] has a one-click "send to coding agent" feature. [[Atlassian Jira|Jira]] has delivery built in. But neither connects customer evidence to executable specs in a way a coding agent can action without human translation.

## Non-YC Companies to Watch

- **[[Trova]]** — most direct response to the RFS. Explicitly positions as "Cursor for Product Managers" with MCP server integration for Cursor/Claude Code. Very early stage.
- **[[BuildBetter]]** — generates evidence-linked PRDs from customer conversations with actual quotes and timestamps. Closest to [[The Full Loop Gap|the full loop]] among non-YC startups.
- **[[Enterpret]]** — $25.1M Series A, strong enterprise traction (Notion, Canva, Linear as clients). Deep feedback analytics.
- **Listen Labs** — $69M Series B, $500M valuation, 1M+ AI interviews. Not YC-funded but in the orbit. Backed by Sequoia.
