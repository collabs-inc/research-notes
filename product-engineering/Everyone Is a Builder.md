# Everyone Is a Builder

The 2026 cultural moment in which job titles drift toward "builder" as AI collapses the distinctions between [[Product Engineer vs Product Manager|PM]], [[Product Engineer vs Software Engineer|engineer]], and designer.

## The Signal

- **SF Standard** (Mar 2026), *"'Engineer' is so 2025. In AI land, everyone's a 'builder' now."* Boris Cherny (creator of Claude Code), Feb 2026: *"Today coding is practically solved… We're going to start to see the title of software engineer go away. It's just going to be 'builder' or 'product manager.'"*
- **[[Guillermo Rauch]]** ([[Vercel]]): *"Everyone's an engineer now"* — the bet to expand builders "from 5 million developers to over 100 million people."
- **[[Amjad Masad]]** ([[Replit]]): not a billion software *engineers* but a billion software *creators* — *"agents all the way down."*

## Title Proliferation

The same force that produced the [[AI Engineer]] (Swyx, 2023) and the [[Forward-Deployed Engineer|forward-deployed engineer]] (Palantir, surging 2025) keeps minting outcome-owner titles: product engineer, design engineer, growth engineer, AI agent orchestrator. LinkedIn reports "60% of fast-growing jobs were new roles enabled by technology." The common thread: roles defined by *outcome ownership + AI fluency* rather than a single technical specialty.

## The Deeper Bet

[[Geoffrey Litt]] ([[Malleable Software]]) and [[Pete Koomen]] push the "builder" frontier past the product team to the *end user* — software that users reshape themselves. In that telling, "product engineering" becomes the discipline of building malleable substrates rather than fixed features.

## The Caution

"Everyone's a builder" is a democratization thesis; [[Charity Majors]] and the [[Will AI Replace Engineers|skeptics]] counter that writing code was always the easy part. Both can be true: the barrier to *making* software collapses while the work of *operating and governing* it does not.
