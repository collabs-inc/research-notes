# 37signals

The maker of Basecamp and HEY, and the most durable example of "big things with small teams" — a [[Small Teams, Big Output|small-team]], no-PM culture codified into a public method, [[Shape Up]].

- **Model:** Teams of designers + programmers ship in **six-week cycles.** No product managers or project managers — "the team is self-organizing." Work is assigned to the team as a whole.
- **Method:** [[Shape Up]] — appetite-driven (fixed time, variable scope), a single "betting table" to prioritize, and *no shared backlog.* Explicitly rejects Scrum's sprints, story points, and backlogs.
- **People:** David Heinemeier Hansson ([[DHH]], creator of Ruby on Rails) and Jason Fried; a deliberately small, opinionated company.

## Why It Matters

37signals is the proof that the [[The Product Engineer|product-engineer]] org pattern — small autonomous teams, no PM layer, full ownership — long predates AI; it's a *cultural* choice that AI now makes *economic*. [[DHH]]'s 2025–26 reversal from AI-coding skeptic to agent-first builder also makes the company a bellwether for how craft-minded engineers adopt agents.
