# Sahil Lavingia

Founder and CEO of [[Gumroad]]; the most-cited practitioner of the radically lean, AI-first org — and a working test of the [[The One-Person Company|one-person company]].

## The Model

Lavingia runs [[Gumroad]] (~$10M+ ARR) with effectively one full-time person (himself) plus a contractor network: no meetings, no deadlines, no full-time employees, everything async, "zero management debt." He reports AI agents writing a large and growing share of Gumroad's commits, and ships features from Slack to production using AI tooling. (Specific percentages and revenue figures come via secondary coverage — treat as approximate.)

His earlier writing documented Gumroad's flat, no-manager structure; the AI-first version is its logical extreme. Ethos: "if it can't be written down, it's not worth doing."

## Why It Matters

Lavingia is the clearest living proof point behind Sam Altman's [[The One-Person Company|one-person company]] bet and the org-design end-state of [[Small Teams, Big Output]] — a profitable software company run by one person, agents, and contractors.
