# Ramp

The corporate-cards / spend platform known for a velocity-driven, engineering-led org with very few PMs — and the cleanest single ratio data point in this vault.

- **The ratio:** Ramp reportedly hit **$100M ARR with fewer than 5 PMs and ~50 engineers** — a striking real-world instance of [[PM-to-Engineer Ratios|a compressed PM:eng ratio]].
- **Model:** Small teams (~5–10 engineers). A **PEDD** structure — Product, Engineering, Design, Data as co-equals, all reporting to the CTO — to keep "Product from becoming stakeholders to Engineering instead of co-founders." Teams organize around customer/business outcomes.
- **Culture:** "Build in the open" (specs, designs, decisions in project Slack channels); "farm for dissent, not approval"; "doing is better than planning."

## Why It Matters

Ramp shows the [[The Product Engineer|product-engineer]] org operating at serious scale and revenue, not just at seed stage — engineers close to outcomes, a thin PM layer, optimized for velocity. It also runs ~15 [[Forward-Deployed Engineer|forward-deployed engineers]] in pods, bridging product and customer.
