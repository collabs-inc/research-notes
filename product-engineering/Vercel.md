# Vercel

The frontend cloud (Next.js, [[v0]]) that institutionalized the [[Design Engineer]] role and became the center of gravity for the [[Craft as a Moat|craft]] movement.

## Design Engineering as a First-Class Role

Vercel's March 2024 post *Design Engineering at Vercel* defined the role as people who "blend aesthetic sensibility with technical skills" to "deeply understand a problem, then design, build, and ship a solution autonomously" — eliminating the design→frontend handoff. Three operating modes: design collaboration, product-team integration, and independent ownership. Vercel "was one of the first companies to hire for 'Design Engineer'," and [[Linear]], [[Stripe]], and [[Notion]] followed. Notable design engineers there include [[Rauno Freiberg]], [[shadcn]], and (formerly) the cohort now at Linear.

## Rauch's Thesis

CEO [[Guillermo Rauch]] frames the mission as expanding builders "from 5 million developers to over 100 million people" — see [[Everyone Is a Builder]]. [[v0]] is positioned as "a design engineer tool." Rauch has cited v0 hitting on the order of ~3,200 PRs merged per day internally (verify framing before quoting). Culture: "Iterate to Greatness," PRs from day two.

## Why It Matters

Vercel turned a fuzzy title into a recruited, compensated, evangelized discipline — and seeded the design-engineer diaspora. It also owns the *ship* end of the [[The Product Engineering Stack|product-engineering stack]] (deploy) and is pulling left into prototype/build via v0.
