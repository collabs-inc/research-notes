# Shopify

The commerce platform whose CEO, [[Tobi Lütke]], issued the era's defining [[The AI Headcount Mandate|AI mandate]] — making it a central case study in how AI reshapes who (and how many) a company hires.

## The Memo (April 2025)

Lütke posted the internal memo publicly (it "was in the process of being leaked"). The verified lines:

- *"Reflexive AI usage is now a baseline expectation at Shopify."*
- *"Before asking for more headcount and resources, teams must demonstrate why they cannot get what they want done using AI."*
- The prompt every team should ask: *"What would this area look like if autonomous AI agents were already part of the team?"*
- AI usage was added to performance and peer-review questionnaires. *"Stagnation is slow-motion failure."*

## Context

Reporting around the memo noted Shopify grew revenue substantially while headcount fell (~11,600 in 2022 toward ~8,100 by end-2024, per secondary summaries — treat as approximate). The memo is the clearest corporate articulation of "prove the work can't be done with AI before adding people," and it set a template other CEOs echoed ([[Amazon]]'s Jassy, Salesforce's Benioff).

## Why It Matters

Shopify converts the [[Small Teams, Big Output|small-team]] thesis from startup culture into large-company policy — the structural forcing function behind the [[The Product Engineer|product-engineer]] shift. See [[The AI Headcount Mandate]].
