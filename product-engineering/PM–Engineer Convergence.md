# PM–Engineer Convergence

The thesis that AI is dissolving the boundary between product management and engineering — that "deciding what to build" and "building it" are merging into a single role, the [[The Product Engineer|product engineer]]. This is the spine of the vault and the premise behind Archetype.

## Asserted From Both Ends

The strongest signal is that credible voices from *three different vantage points* converge on the same conclusion:

- **Builder side** — [[Austen Allred]] (Feb 2025): *"I don't see how the roles of 'product manager' and 'engineer' don't get mashed together in the future. Totally reasonable for a person to figure out what to build and build it with AI."*
- **Engineering-media side** — [[Gergely Orosz]]: AI makes "the practices that the best eng teams did until now, the baseline" — explicitly naming "product-minded engineers" as that baseline.
- **PM-thought-leadership side** — [[Marty Cagan]]: PMs who merely "aggregate requests, generate roadmaps, and create PRDs" are doing work "an agent — or your engineer or designer — could just as easily do themselves."

## The Mechanism

Convergence runs in both directions: AI makes engineers more product-minded (they can prototype and ship product decisions without a PM relay) and PMs more technical (they can prototype, query data, and [[Vibe Coding|vibe code]] without an engineer). The handoff chain — PM decides → designer designs → engineer codes — becomes a bottleneck when any one person can do all three with AI. See [[PM-to-Engineer Ratios]] for the quantitative version.

## The Counter-Reading

This is reshaping, not deletion. PM hiring is at multi-year highs ([[Will AI Replace Product Managers]]); [[PostHog]] keeps PMs as context-providers. The honest claim: the *handoff* is collapsing and the *artifacts* (PRDs, roadmaps) are commoditizing, while product *judgment* concentrates — into whoever is closest to building.
