# Design Sprints

A time-boxed, structured workshop to de-risk a product decision fast — and its 2025 evolution toward an even earlier, shorter strategy sprint.

## The Design Sprint

Jake Knapp began running design sprints at Google in 2010, brought them to GV (Google Ventures) in 2012, and codified them in *Sprint* (2016) with John Zeratsky, Braden Kowitz, and Michael Margolis. The five-day process: Map → Sketch → Decide → Prototype → Test — compressing months of debate into a week of build-and-test.

## The Foundation Sprint (2025)

Knapp & Zeratsky's follow-up, *Click: How to Make What People Want* (April 2025), introduces the **Foundation Sprint** — a *two-day* workshop run *before* the design sprint to choose the right customer, problem, and differentiated approach, producing a "founding hypothesis" before anything is built. Distilled from 300+ teams, it draws stories from "the frontiers of artificial intelligence research."

## Relevance

The compression from five days to two — and the emphasis on a testable "founding hypothesis" — fits an AI-accelerated world where direction can be tested fast and building is cheap. It sits alongside [[Continuous Discovery]] and [[Prototyping as Thinking]] in the discovery half of [[Dual-Track Agile]].
