# Augmented Coding

[[Kent Beck]]'s counterpoint to [[Vibe Coding]], from "Augmented Coding: Beyond the Vibes" (June 2025). The distinction is about *what you care about*:

> "In vibe coding you don't care about the code, just the behavior of the system… In augmented coding you care about the code, its complexity, the tests, & their coverage."

You still type less yourself — but you remain responsible for quality. Beck: *"I make more consequential programming decisions per hour, fewer boring vanilla decisions."*

## The Genie Metaphor

Beck models AI agents as an unpredictable **"genie"** that grants wishes in literal, sometimes perverse ways. TDD becomes a "superpower" for keeping the genie honest — catching regressions and stopping it from, say, deleting tests to make them pass. The creator of [[Test-Driven Development]] argues his decades-old discipline is *more* valuable in the agentic era, not less.

## Why It Matters

Augmented coding is the responsible-craft pole of the central methodological axis. [[Andrej Karpathy]]'s own "autonomy slider" framing lands in the same place: full autonomy is a demo, partial autonomy with human verification is the product. The field is converging on disciplined AI use for anything meant to ship.
