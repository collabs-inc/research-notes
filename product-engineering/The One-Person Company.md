# The One-Person Company

The asymptote of [[PM–Engineer Convergence]], [[Small Teams, Big Output|small teams]], and [[The Generalist|generalism]]: a single person, armed with AI, running a company that once needed dozens.

## The Canonical Quote

**Sam Altman**, in conversation with Alexis Ohanian (reported by Fortune, Feb 2024): *"In my little group chat with my tech CEO friends there's this betting pool for the first year that there is a one-person billion-dollar company. Which would have been unimaginable without AI and now will happen."* He has separately floated the idea of a startup as "one person and 10,000 GPUs."

## The Working Proof Points

- **[[Gumroad]] / [[Sahil Lavingia]]:** ~$10M+ ARR run with effectively one full-time person plus a contractor network — "zero management debt."
- **[[Magic Patterns]]:** reached $1M ARR with "zero full-time employees" before raising.
- **[[Midjourney]]:** ~$200M+ revenue with a famously tiny team and *no outside venture capital.*

These are not literal one-person billion-dollar companies, but they show the curve bending. TechCrunch (Feb 2025) framed the broader question: "AI agents could birth the first one-person unicorn — but at what societal cost?"

## Why It Matters Here

The one-person company is the limit of the [[The Product Engineer|product-engineer]] thesis — the same person does product, design, engineering, support, and growth. It is also the cleanest argument for why a tool that amplifies *product thinking* (not just code generation) is valuable: when one person is the whole company, judgment is the scarce resource.
