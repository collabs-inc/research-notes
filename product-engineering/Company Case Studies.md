# Company Case Studies

A hub for the organizations that show [[The Product Engineer|product-engineer]] culture in practice. The recurring pattern: **small, autonomous, single-owner teams; few or no PMs; an extreme hiring bar; and [[The Generalist|generalists]] over specialists** — increasingly amplified by AI.

## Product-Engineer / No-PM Orgs

- [[PostHog]] — the archetype; "one-pizza teams," engineers own product
- [[Linear]] — taste-driven, ~2 PMs, ephemeral project pods
- [[37signals]] — [[Shape Up]], six-week cycles, no PMs
- [[Ramp]] — $100M ARR with <5 PMs and ~50 engineers
- [[Stripe]] — "the more technical the product, the fewer PMs"
- [[Retool]] — all-engineer founding team, builders as users

## Craft / Design-Engineering Orgs

- [[Vercel]] — institutionalized the [[Design Engineer]] role
- [[Figma]] — permeable design/eng membrane; craft as the moat
- [[Superhuman]] — craft and quality as strategy
- [[Notion]] — generalists; designers who code

## AI-Amplified Lean Orgs

- [[Anthropic]] — "mini-PMs"; "hire more PMs, not engineers"
- [[OpenAI]] — technical PMs; FDE team
- [[Anysphere (Cursor)]] — extreme [[Revenue Per Employee]]
- [[Midjourney]] — tiny team, no VC
- [[Gumroad]] — contractor-only, AI-first ([[Sahil Lavingia]])
- [[Replit]] — agent-driven, lean
- [[Bending Spoons]] — generalist portfolio operator

## Org Primitives

- [[Amazon]] — two-pizza teams, single-threaded ownership, [[Working Backwards]]
- [[Shopify]] — the [[The AI Headcount Mandate|AI headcount mandate]]
