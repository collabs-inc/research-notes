# The Product Engineer

The central concept of this vault. A **product engineer** is a software developer who builds products for real users, writes full-stack code, makes product decisions, and owns outcomes — a hybrid of [[Product Engineer vs Software Engineer|software engineer]] and [[Product Engineer vs Product Manager|product manager]]. The defining trait, repeated across every credible definition, is *owning the outcome rather than the implementation*.

## Two Origins, Both ~2019

The title and the mindset were named almost simultaneously:

- **The title** — Sherif Mansour and Jean-Michel Lemieux (then Atlassian's head of engineering) popularized "product engineer" in Mansour's April 2019 essay *Product Engineers* (Atlassian Product Craft blog). Lemieux's definition: engineers *"who have a thirst for using technologies to leapfrog human/user problems… with empathy to reach for magical experiences."* The term predates this — secondary sources place it in hiring since ~2016 — but Atlassian popularized it.
- **The mindset** — Gergely Orosz's September 2019 essay [[The Product-Minded Software Engineer]] codified the *traits* a year before the AI wave: curiosity about "why," product/eng tradeoffs offered upfront, fast validation, end-to-end ownership.

See [[Gergely Orosz]] for the lineage.

## The Consensus Definition

Strikingly stable across sources:

- **[[PostHog]]:** *"A software developer who builds products for real users, writes full-stack code, and deeply cares about solving critical user problems."* Engineers own pricing, revenue, and UX — not just code.
- **[[Lee Robinson]]** (now Cursor; then Vercel), Aug 2023: product engineers *"work backwards from the desired product experience to the set of technologies that enable it."*
- **incident.io:** they *"care more about outcomes and impact than the exact implementation, or the tools used to solve the problem."*

The role is defined as much by what it *removes* — the [[PM–Engineer Convergence|PM→design→eng handoff]] — as by what it adds.

## Why Now

The concept predates AI (Hacker News "Who's Hiring?" mentions of "product engineer" roughly doubled, ~2%→5%, by Feb 2024 per Vipul Shekhawat's analysis; [[PostHog]] cites an 89% rise in searches since 2021). But AI is the accelerant: when implementation cost collapses, [[Taste as the Bottleneck|judgment about what to build]] becomes the scarce, defensible input. This is the thesis underneath [[PM–Engineer Convergence]] and the whole [[Everyone Is a Builder]] moment — and it is the user Archetype is built for.
