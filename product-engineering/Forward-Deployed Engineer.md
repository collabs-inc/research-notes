# Forward-Deployed Engineer

An engineer embedded in a customer's team to close the gap between what a product does and what a specific customer needs. **Coined by Palantir** (originally "Deltas"); until ~2016 Palantir had more FDEs than software engineers. a16z called it *"the hottest job in tech."*

## The Distinction

Gergely Orosz's framing (Pragmatic Engineer, Aug 2025): the FDE is *"one customer, many capabilities"* versus the traditional engineer's *"one capability, many customers."* [[PostHog]] puts it sharply: *"product engineers aim to solve a problem that many people have so they can create a reusable solution, while FDEs are focused on one customer at a time."* FDEs spend 60–80% of their time with the customer, build rough prototypes, and travel heavily. Bob McGrew's metaphor: FDEs *"build the rough gravel road,"* core engineers make it *"a paved superhighway."*

This also distinguishes it from a **solutions/sales engineer** (pre-sales, demos, POCs) — the FDE ships real product code at the customer.

## The Breakout Title of 2025–26

The FDE went from Palantir jargon to one of tech's fastest-growing roles:

- Postings **+800% Jan–Sep 2025; +1,165% YoY** (Live Data Technologies, via Paraform), while the candidate pool grew only ~50% — an acute supply gap. Indeed data corroborates a jump from ~643 (Apr 2025) to ~5,300+ (Apr 2026).
- **Compensation:** median base ~$174K; founding FDE base $166K–$266K; staff/principal $190K–$288K.
- **Concentration:** ~83% of roles in SF or NY; 59% at seed–Series A. Hiring led by [[OpenAI]] (built an FDE team in early 2025, "started with 2, now 10+") and [[Anthropic]], then Palantir, Cognition, [[Ramp]] (~15 FDEs), Decagon.

## Why It Matters Here

The FDE is the *enterprise-facing* cousin of the product engineer — both collapse roles and ship end-to-end, both surge for the same reason: AI makes a single high-agency builder able to span discovery, build, and delivery. See [[Labor Market]] and [[Everyone Is a Builder]].
