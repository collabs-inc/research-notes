# Thariq Shihipar

The Anthropic engineer (on Claude Code) whose May 2026 thread "The Unreasonable Effectiveness of HTML" crystallized the thesis into a slogan: **"HTML is the new Markdown."** Previously co-founded a YC startup and worked at the MIT Media Lab.

## The Argument

Posted 8 May 2026 (companion site `thariqs.github.io/html-effectiveness` with ~20 self-contained example files; reported reach in the millions of views). The case: as agents got more powerful, [[Markdown]] became "a restricting format"; finished deliverables — plans, specs, reports, PR writeups — should be HTML because they're denser, survive length, share as native links, and become two-way interactive. The clincher is that **HTML gets read**: a rendered, navigable artifact beats a wall of text. See [[HTML Is the New Markdown]] for the five-part argument and carve-outs.

## The Deeper Framing

In follow-up interviews, Thariq frames HTML artifacts as **"disposable user interfaces"** — "micro-software on top of micro-software" — and notes that only ~1% of generated tokens become production code, so spending tokens on rich communication artifacts is worth it. He describes humans becoming **"compute allocators"** deciding what merits the model's resources, and HTML design systems as **"portable sources of truth"** that travel with code. See [[The Disposable Artifact]].

## Why It Matters

Thariq supplies the canonical, dated articulation of the vault's thesis — from inside the lab whose [[Claude Artifacts]] mainstreamed HTML output. His piece converted even [[Simon Willison]].
