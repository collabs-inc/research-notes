# Stripe

The payments-infrastructure company that supplies the clearest *principle* for why developer-facing products run lean on PMs.

## The Rule

From Stripe's own Atlas guidance: *"The more technical and backend heavy your product, the fewer product managers you will have."* A developer/API company runs a much lower [[PM-to-Engineer Ratios|PM:eng ratio]] than a consumer-internet company, because engineers are themselves close to the user and the product surface is technical.

- **Culture:** Engineers are expected to be "close to users and involved in the process end-to-end," even as PM count grew with product breadth. Customer-immersion and writing culture are emphasized (Stripe is famous for prizing clear writing — see [[Working Backwards]]).

## Why It Matters

Stripe generalizes the [[PostHog]]/[[Linear]] pattern into a rule of thumb: the more technical the product, the more the [[The Product Engineer|product engineer]] absorbs the PM role. For Archetype's day-one users — technical founders and PM/engineers building developer-adjacent products — this is the most directly applicable principle in the vault.
