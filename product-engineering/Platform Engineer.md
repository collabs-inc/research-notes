# Platform Engineer

The internal-facing counterpart to the [[The Product Engineer|product engineer]]. [[Lee Robinson]]'s 2023 product-vs-platform split is the canonical framing:

- **Product engineers** build customer-facing features and optimize for iteration velocity and UX.
- **Platform engineers** build the internal infrastructure, tooling, and developer platforms (IDPs) that make product engineers productive — optimizing for developer productivity, scalability, and reliability.

Robinson's claim: *"Increasingly, a small but effective group of Platform Engineers can make incredibly impactful decisions, even for smaller teams."* In this model an org has product engineers and platform engineers — and comparatively few pure "software engineers."

## Why It Matters

Platform engineering is the part of the stack that resists the [[The Generalist|generalist]] sweep. As [[Charity Majors]] argues, operating, understanding, and governing systems over their lifecycle stays a specialist domain even as writing code commoditizes — see [[Will AI Replace Engineers]]. The product/platform divide is one of the cleaner fault lines in the [[The Role and Its Variants|role taxonomy]].
