# AI Engineer

The adjacent new title that established the *pattern* the [[The Product Engineer|product engineer]] now follows: when a foundation model commoditizes a hard skill, a generalist "engineer-who-ships" emerges between the specialists and the end users.

## Origin: "The Rise of the AI Engineer"

Swyx (Shawn Wang), Latent Space, June 2023. The supply argument: ~5,000 LLM researchers versus ~50 million software engineers globally → an "in-between" class of AI Engineers must rise to meet demand. At the time there were ~10× more "ML Engineer" than "AI Engineer" jobs on Indeed; Swyx predicted the ratio would invert within five years. The AI Engineer Summit (Oct 2023) sold out instantly. *"When it comes to shipping AI products, you want engineers, not researchers."*

## The Labor Data (the role's cousin with hard numbers)

- **#1 on LinkedIn's "Jobs on the Rise" 2025**; fastest-growing job for young workers two years running. LinkedIn added ~639K AI-related US postings 2023–25, ~75K of them AI Engineer roles.
- **Compensation** (levels.fyi, 2025): US average ~$245K; the AI premium *grows with seniority* — Entry +6.2%, Engineer +11.9%, Senior +14.2%, Staff +18.7%. Company medians: Meta ~$378K, Microsoft ~$282K, Google ~$280K.

## Why It Matters Here

The product engineer is "the same Swyx pattern applied to *product judgment* instead of *ML*." See [[Everyone Is a Builder]] for the broader title-proliferation trend and [[Labor Market]] for how product-engineer demand compares.
