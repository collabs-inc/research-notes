# The Product-Minded Software Engineer

The canonical essay defining the *mindset* that the title [[The Product Engineer|"product engineer"]] later formalized. Written by [[Gergely Orosz]], published September 5, 2019 (updated Feb 2022) on the Pragmatic Engineer blog.

## Definition

> "developers with lots of interest in the product itself. They want to understand why decisions are made, how people use the product, and love to be involved in making product decisions."

## The Traits Orosz Lists

1. Proactive with product ideas/opinions — don't just take a spec and implement
2. Interest in the business, user behavior, and the data on it
3. Curiosity, a keen interest in "why?"
4. Strong communicators with great relationships with non-engineers
5. Offering product/engineering tradeoffs upfront
6. Pragmatic handling of edge cases — the "Minimum Lovable Concept"
7. Quick product validation cycles before building production-ready
8. End-to-end feature ownership, staying close to data and CX after launch
9. Strong product instincts built through repeated cycles

> "Juggling both the product and engineering tradeoffs and the impact of each is a unique strength product-minded engineers have."

## Why It Matters

This is the foundational text of the [[The Product Engineer|product-engineer]] lineage. Nearly every later definition — [[PostHog]], [[Lee Robinson]], incident.io — echoes these traits. The 2019 essay contains no AI; the mindset is older than the tooling.

In 2025 the concept became an O'Reilly book, *The Product-Minded Engineer*, by Drew Hoskins, who frames it for "AI-native" teams. And in the AI era Orosz argues the practices of the best teams — including product-mindedness — become the *baseline* for everyone (see [[Will AI Replace Engineers]]).
