# DHH

David Heinemeier Hansson — creator of Ruby on Rails; co-owner and CTO of [[37signals]] (Basecamp, HEY). Notable here for a high-profile *reversal* on AI coding.

## The Arc

- **Early 2025 (skeptic):** On the Lex Fridman podcast, DHH argued AI tools couldn't match a junior programmer, likened them to a "flickering" light bulb, and worried that not typing code himself was eroding his own learning — criticizing Cursor/Windsurf for undermining the learning process. (Exact wording via secondary coverage — verify before quoting.)
- **Late 2025 / early 2026 (convert):** He did "a complete 180," going agent-first, citing terminal harnesses (Claude Code / OpenCode) plus a late-2025 model jump as the inflection point ("Promoting AI agents"; documented by [[Gergely Orosz]] in "DHH's new way of writing code").

## Why It Matters

DHH is a bellwether for how craft-minded, opinionated engineers adopt agents — and his reversal tracks the broader 2025→2026 shift from [[Vibe Coding|skepticism]] to [[Augmented Coding|disciplined agent use]]. As the author of [[Shape Up]]'s home company, his practices carry weight in the [[Small Teams, Big Output|small-team]] world.
