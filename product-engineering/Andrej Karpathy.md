# Andrej Karpathy

Founder of Eureka Labs; ex-Director of AI at Tesla and founding member of OpenAI. The dominant vocabulary-setter for this whole discourse.

## What He Named

- **[[Vibe Coding|"Vibe coding"]]** (Feb 2025): *"a new kind of coding… where you fully give in to the vibes… and forget that the code even exists."*
- **"Software 1.0 / 2.0 / 3.0"** (YC AI Startup School keynote, June 2025): 1.0 = humans write code; 2.0 = humans curate datasets and train neural nets; 3.0 = humans program LLMs in natural language. Hence *"the hottest new programming language is English."*
- **Partial autonomy / "autonomy sliders":** he argues for human-in-the-loop augmentation ("Iron-Man suit") over flashy full-autonomy demos. *"Demo is works.any(), product is works.all()."* And on shipping: "you are not allowed to introduce vulnerabilities because of vibe coding."

He calls 2025–2035 "the decade of agents."

## Why It Matters

Karpathy supplies the *framing* that makes [[Taste as the Bottleneck|taste]] matter: when "English is the programming language," the scarce skill is knowing what to say and what to keep. His autonomy-slider stance also legitimizes the [[Augmented Coding|disciplined]] middle ground between full automation and hand-coding — the working mode of the [[The Product Engineer|product engineer]].
